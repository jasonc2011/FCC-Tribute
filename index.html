Takashi Amano

Photographer, Designer, Aquarist

Takashi Amano, center, overlooks the design and construction of his final masterpiece the 40 meter long, 160 thousand litre Nature Aquarium "Forests Underwater" at the Lisbon Aquarium.

Considered the Father of Modern Aquarium Design, Amano employed Japanese gardening concepts such as Wabi-sabi and Zen rock arrangement. His tank compositions sought to mimic nature in their appearance. Amano also pioneered the extensive use of Glossostigma elatinoides and Riccia fluitans as plant material, and used shrimp as a means of controlling the growth of algae.

in Niigata, Japan in 1954, Amano first was known for his beautiful work photographing untouched tropical rainforests in the Amazon, Borneo, and West Africa. He used what he learned observing these environments to develop his ground breaking Nature Aquarium Style of aquascaping.

He founded Aqua Design Amano Co., Ltd. in 1982, providing aquatic plant growing equipment. His photo books of what he called the "Nature Aquarium", no Naka no Daishizen, published in 1992, followed by eno kaiki, were translated into 7 languages.

"What is the essence of a beautiful landscape? The compositional beauty produced by flora and other things may be part of it, but isn't it ultimately a perfectly functioning ecosystem? It is, in fact, a balance. The world of aquarium is no exception.....'what type of environment would I prefer if I were a fish' was always at the base when the layouts were created." - Takashi Amano
